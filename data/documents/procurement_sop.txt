PROCUREMENT STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Horizon Enterprises
Version 3.0 | Effective: March 2024
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. PURPOSE
━━━━━━━━━━
This SOP defines the procurement process for all goods and services purchased on behalf of Horizon Enterprises. It establishes approval hierarchies, documentation requirements, and vendor management standards to ensure financial governance.

2. SCOPE
━━━━━━━━
This SOP applies to all procurement activities including software licenses, hardware, consulting services, office supplies, and third-party vendor contracts. It applies to all departments and employees initiating purchase requests.

3. PROCUREMENT CATEGORIES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
3.1 Category A — Routine Supplies (below Rs. 5,000)
These include stationery, minor office supplies, and consumables. These may be purchased directly by the requesting department without a formal purchase order. Receipt and documentation must be submitted to Finance within 5 working days.

3.2 Category B — Standard Purchases (Rs. 5,001 to Rs. 25,000)
Requires a formal Purchase Request (PR) submitted via the procurement portal. Must include item description, quantity, estimated cost, and business justification. Finance department review required before vendor contact.

3.3 Category C — High Value Purchases (Rs. 25,001 to Rs. 1,00,000)
Requires a minimum of 3 vendor quotations. A comparative statement must be submitted with the PR. Vendor selection must be justified based on cost, quality, and delivery timelines.

3.4 Category D — Strategic Purchases (above Rs. 1,00,000)
Requires a full Request for Proposal (RFP) process. Vendor evaluation committee must be constituted. Legal review of contracts is mandatory before signing.

4. APPROVAL AUTHORITY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
The following approval hierarchy applies to all procurement requests:

4.1 Up to Rs. 10,000
Approved by: Direct Department Manager
Documentation: Purchase Request form with cost estimate
Timeline: Approval within 2 working days

4.2 Rs. 10,001 to Rs. 50,000
Approved by: Department Head
Documentation: PR form + minimum 2 vendor quotes
Timeline: Approval within 3 working days

4.3 Rs. 50,001 to Rs. 1,00,000
Approved by: Finance Manager AND Department Head jointly
Documentation: PR form + 3 vendor quotes + comparative analysis
Timeline: Approval within 5 working days

4.4 Above Rs. 1,00,000
Approved by: Chief Financial Officer (CFO) AND Managing Director (MD) jointly
Documentation: RFP document + vendor evaluation report + legal review
Timeline: Approval within 10 working days
No vendor commitment may be made before dual approval is received in writing.

5. PURCHASE REQUEST PROCESS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Step 1: Requestor logs into the procurement portal and submits a Purchase Request (PR) with all mandatory fields.
Step 2: System automatically routes the PR to the appropriate approver based on amount.
Step 3: Approver reviews and approves, rejects, or requests clarification within the stipulated timeline.
Step 4: Approved PR triggers a Purchase Order (PO) generation by Finance.
Step 5: PO is sent to the selected vendor. No verbal purchase commitments are permitted.
Step 6: Goods/services received. Requestor confirms receipt in the portal within 2 working days of delivery.
Step 7: Invoice matched to PO and GRN (Goods Receipt Note) by Finance for payment processing.

6. ESCALATION PROCEDURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
6.1 Delayed Approvals
If an approver does not act on a PR within the stipulated timeline:
- Day 1 beyond deadline: Automated reminder sent to approver.
- Day 2 beyond deadline: Escalation email sent to approver's manager and procurement team.
- Day 3 beyond deadline: Finance head is notified and a manual review is initiated.

6.2 Emergency Procurement
In situations requiring immediate procurement (system failure, critical operations), the requesting department head may initiate emergency procurement with verbal approval from CFO, followed by written confirmation within 24 hours. Emergency procurement is subject to post-hoc audit.

6.3 Dispute Resolution
Disputes between requestor and approver regarding procurement decisions must be escalated to the Finance Head. If unresolved, the matter is referred to the MD's office.

7. VENDOR MANAGEMENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
All vendors must be onboarded in the vendor master system before a PO can be raised. Vendor onboarding requires: GST certificate, PAN card, bank account details, and completion of vendor KYC form. New vendor onboarding takes 3-5 working days.

8. COMPLIANCE AND AUDIT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
All procurement activities are subject to internal audit on a quarterly basis. Non-compliance with this SOP may result in disciplinary action. Employees must retain all procurement documents for a minimum of 5 years.
